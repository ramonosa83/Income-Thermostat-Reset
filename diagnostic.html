The Thermostat Diagnostic

Ten minutes. Three questions. First answers are true answers.

One instruction before you start: answer with your body in the room. If a question makes something tighten, note where. That's data — the best data on this page.

01The Number

Look back over the last few years. Not your best month. Not your worst. The number your income keeps returning to — the one it snaps back to after every spike, like a thermostat clicking on.

My income keeps returning to:

If you're not sure, write the number that came to mind first. That was it. Business owners: use what you actually pay yourself, not revenue — the thermostat regulates what YOU receive.

02The Brake

Now imagine next month comes in meaningfully above that number. Not fantasy money — real, plausible, above your line. Sit with that picture for ten full seconds before you write.

What just happened in your body? Where — throat, chest, stomach, shoulders, jaw?

Whatever you just felt — that's the brake. You just caught it in the act. And if you felt nothing at all — a blankness, a fog, "I can't even picture it" — write exactly that. Numbness is a brake too. It's the sneakiest one.

03The Installation

This is the deep one. Take your time.

What did having “more than enough” cost someone in your family?

Who paid a price for money, success, or standing out — and what was the price? Resentment? A lawsuit? Being targeted, envied, isolated? A marriage? Their health? Who got punished for having, or punished for wanting?

If a specific person and a specific story surfaced before you finished reading the question — write that one. Your body brought it up for a reason.

Reading Your Own Answers

Put the three together and you're looking at something almost nobody ever sees written down: your set-point, your alarm system, and the event that installed it.

Question 1 is the setting.

Your system defends this number — above it AND below it, which is why bad months mysteriously recover too.

Question 2 is the alarm.

That physical signature is what “dangerous” feels like in your body. When you feel it now, you'll know what it is — which changes your relationship to it forever.

Question 3 is the installation.

Your thermostat wasn't set randomly. Someone paid a price for “more,” your body was in the room, and it decided: not us. Never us. It's been keeping that promise ever since — out of love, believe it or not.

One thing NOT to do with this page: don't argue with it. Don't affirm at it. The part that set the thermostat doesn't speak that language. It speaks the language of felt safety — which is exactly where we're going next.

Your answers save automatically on this device. The copy button puts all three on your clipboard — paste them anywhere you want to keep them.

Keep your number in mind. Bring it with you into the Safety Switch.
